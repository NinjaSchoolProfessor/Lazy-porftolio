## Tax-Location Strategy

### Covered Call ETF Placement

| ETF | Account Type | Why |
|---|---|---|
| SPYI | Taxable (JWROS) | Section 1256 contracts: 60% long-term / 40% short-term tax treatment. ROC distributions defer taxes. Most tax-efficient option for taxable accounts. |
| JEPQ | Roth IRA | Distributions taxed as ordinary income — irrelevant in Roth (tax-free). Higher yield from Nasdaq-100 volatility. |
| JEPI | Traditional IRA | Distributions taxed as ordinary income — irrelevant in Trad IRA (tax-deferred). Broader S&P 500 exposure. |
| FEPI | Any | Small position (<1%), minimal tax impact regardless of placement. |

### General Tax-Location Principles
- **Roth IRA / HSA:** Highest-growth assets (SMH, XLK) — tax-free growth on biggest compounders
- **Traditional IRA:** Income-generating assets (JEPI, AMLP, TLT) — defer ordinary income
- **Taxable (JWROS):** Tax-efficient funds (SPYI, VXUS, IAU) — Section 1256 treatment, foreign tax credit, low turnover
- **Cash (SWVXX/SPAXX):** Sweep idle settlement cash in all accounts — earn ~3% risk-free

## Executive Summary
This portfolio is designed to capture the next leg of the AI cycle while staying diversified enough to hold through volatility. Updated in March 2026 to reflect a defensive rotation in response to three converging macro shocks: the Israel-Iran conflict and resulting oil price spike above $118/bbl, a hawkish Fed hold with rising inflation projections, and broad equity market weakness with the S&P 500 hitting 2026 lows.

The rebalance shifts weight from concentrated tech/semiconductor positions into defensive income generators (covered call ETFs, midstream energy), macro hedges (gold, long-duration Treasuries), and maintains significant cash reserves (~22%) earning ~3% for opportunistic deployment.

The core AI thesis remains intact — Nvidia's GTC 2026 confirmed $1T+ in chip orders through 2027 and Micron's record HBM guidance validates the AI capex supercycle — but position sizing now reflects the heightened near-term risks from energy-driven inflation and geopolitical uncertainty.

**Key themes through mid-2026:**
- **Stagflation defense:** IAU (gold), AMLP (midstream), XLU (utilities), XLV (healthcare) provide inflation protection and non-cyclical income
- **Income generation:** SPYI/JEPQ/JEPI covered call overlay extracts premium from elevated VIX in a range-bound market
- **Recession hedge:** TLT provides convexity if the oil shock tips the economy into contraction and forces Fed cuts
- **AI cycle participation:** SMH + XLK maintained at reduced weights for when the cycle reaccelerates
- **Optionality:** 22% cash earning ~3% preserves dry powder for opportunistic deployment at lower prices

## AI Impact on Major Market Sectors (Last 6 Months)

### Technology & Semiconductors
AI has accounted for roughly 60% of recent U.S. economic growth. Hyperscalers (Microsoft, Alphabet, Amazon, Meta) pledged over $300 billion in AI data center and GPU investments. Nvidia's GTC 2026 confirmed over $1 trillion in cumulative orders for Blackwell and Vera Rubin AI chips through 2027. Micron reported record HBM demand with 2026 capacity sold out and a 30% dividend hike. However, the sector faces near-term headwinds from rising energy costs, potential supply chain disruptions to chipmaking materials (helium, bromine) from the Middle East conflict, and broader risk-off sentiment. Software stocks have been hit particularly hard — the iShares Expanded Tech-Software Sector ETF (IGV) has fallen roughly 30% from its September 2025 peak. Stock correlations within AI infrastructure dropped from 80% to 20% since June 2025, signaling increased selectivity.

### Energy & Utilities
The Israel-Iran conflict has transformed the energy landscape. Brent crude spiked past $118/bbl with the Strait of Hormuz effectively closed to tanker traffic. The IEA projects global oil supply to plunge by 8 mb/d in March 2026 — the largest supply disruption in oil market history. U.S. midstream operators (AMLP) are direct beneficiaries — higher domestic production volumes flow through their fee-based pipeline networks while European and Asian buyers scramble for U.S. LNG. Utilities (XLU) have emerged as a rare bright spot, offering both defensive characteristics and structural AI power demand growth. Nuclear energy momentum continues with Bank of America projecting 18 GW of new capacity annually through 2040, positioning nuclear as a potential $10 trillion industry.

### Industrials
Industrials face a mixed outlook. Defense spending is surging with active Middle East conflict, benefiting XAR directly. Infrastructure buildout (PAVE) remains supported by long-term AI data center and reshoring investment. However, rising energy costs compress margins for energy-intensive manufacturers, and the broader industrial cycle is vulnerable to demand slowdown if the oil shock persists. Schwab previously rated Industrials as 'Outperform' — this call is now under review given the energy cost headwind.

### Healthcare
Healthcare (XLV) is proving its defensive value during the March 2026 selloff, holding near flat while the broader market drops 4-5%. AI-assisted drug discovery and diagnostics continue to accelerate, and the sector's non-cyclical demand provides portfolio ballast in a stagflationary environment. Valuations remain compressed relative to the broader market, creating an attractive entry point.

### Small & Mid Caps
Small caps (IJR) and mid caps (IJH) are under significant pressure, down 7% YTD. However, this creates attractive entry points for staged deployment. The S&P 600 is still projected to deliver 19-21% earnings growth in 2026, and small caps trade at ~17x P/E versus 29x for the S&P 500 — a 22% discount near historic lows. A resolution of Middle East tensions or Fed pivot would catalyze a sharp rebound. Cash reserves are earmarked for deployment into these positions if the S&P 500 reaches 6,400-6,500.

### International Equities
International equities (VXUS) are under renewed pressure from the energy shock. Europe is particularly vulnerable given its near-total dependence on energy imports — European equities fell 2% on March 19 alone. Asian markets dropped 2.8% following the Fed decision. Single-country bets (EPU, EWY, EZU) were eliminated from the portfolio as they added concentrated risk without adequate compensation. The core VXUS position is retained for long-term diversification but is not being added to in the current environment.

## 5-Year Forward Outlook (2026-2031)

### AI Capex Cycle & Revenue Monetization
BlackRock projects $5-8 trillion in cumulative AI-related capital expenditure through 2030. Goldman Sachs consensus puts 2026 hyperscaler capex at $527 billion alone. Nvidia's GTC 2026 confirms the supply side remains robust — $1T+ in orders for next-generation AI chips. The critical inflection point: returns shift from 'who can build fastest' to 'who can drive the highest revenue per dollar of AI infrastructure.' This broadening means AI returns will spread across all 11 GICS sectors, not just technology.

### GDP & Productivity Impact
PwC estimates AI could expand global GDP by nearly 15% by 2035, adding more than 1 percentage point of growth annually — comparable to 19th-century industrialization. Industries combining data intensity with operational complexity (healthcare, manufacturing, financial services) are best positioned. PwC's 2025 AI Jobs Barometer found that AI-exposed industries show nearly 3x higher revenue-per-employee growth than less exposed industries.

### Energy & Geopolitical Risk
The Israel-Iran conflict introduces a new structural variable. Even if resolved in 2026, the demonstration that the Strait of Hormuz can be effectively closed reprices global energy risk permanently. This accelerates three trends: (1) U.S. energy independence and domestic production growth, benefiting midstream infrastructure (AMLP); (2) nuclear energy investment as baseload alternative, supporting GRID and XLU; (3) defense spending escalation globally, supporting XAR. Oil price volatility may persist for years even after the current conflict, adding a permanent inflation risk premium.

### Key Risks
An MIT report in July 2025 found that 95% of generative AI pilots fail to achieve measurable P&L impact. The AI builders are leveraging up — investment is front-loaded while revenues are back-loaded, creating a more levered financial system vulnerable to shocks. Valuations for mega-cap tech remain stretched; the S&P 500's equity risk premium hit just 0.02%, among the lowest on record. The Middle East conflict adds a new tail risk: prolonged energy supply disruption could trigger a global recession that overwhelms even AI-driven productivity gains.
